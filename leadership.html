CLUBS

I'm very proud to say I was given the oppurtunity to lead one of the clubs that I've been a part of for quite a while at my school! Read more below:

FEMINISM IS FOR EVERYBODY

I co-lead the feminism club at my school. We meet once a week, but our work extends beyond the classroom we meet in. Whether it's bringing the conversations that emerge from that week's topic or documentary back to our homes and friends, or putting up messages about rape culture on school bulletin boards, the work we do is difficult, transformative, and necessary.

DRIVES AND BAKESALES

At my time so far at LREI, I've led multiple school-wide drives. Check them out:

HOLIDAY DRIVE

The winter of my freshman year, three of my friends and I ran a Holiday Drive for children at the NYU's Hospital for Joint Diseases.

YELLOWHAMMER FUND

During the spring of my sophomore year, in response to the abortion rights being rolled back across the country my feminist club held a bake sake in our school's front lobby. All of proceeds went to the Yellowhammer fund, an organization in Alabama (the state where the most restictive bill was passed) that provides funding for anyone seeking an abortion in one of Alabama's three clinics.

SANITARY PRODUCT DRIVE

Towards the end of the first trimester of my junior year, LREI partook in a menstrual drive along with sixteen other private schools in New York City. Along with a friend, I repped the drive at LREI. After putting up posters, boxes, sending out emails and making announcements, when the schools came together we had a collective number of 10,000 pads and tampons to send to Cruzando Fronteras, an organization that aids families on the Mexican side of the U.S.-Mexican border while they await movement on their US immigration and asylum pensions.

DIVERSITY

I've been lucky enough to have been able to take part in multiple diversity initiatives outside of my school, regionally and nationally.

DAIS

Since the May of my sophomore year, I have been a part of the DAIS community, which stands for the Diveristy Awareness Initiative for Students. We meet monthly with students from various New York City private schools to have conversations varying topics, such as stereotypes and our experience at our schools, intent vs. impact within social media, and generational experiences of the youth, to name a few.

SDLC

During December of my junior year, four other students and I were selected to represent our school at the 2019 Student Diversity Leadership Conference (SDLC), which was being held in Seattle, Washington that year. Students who attend private schools from all over the United States and a select other countries got together and spoke about our experiences at our private schools, noticing the similarities in our plights and brainstorming ways to bring back what we learned and transforming it into change at our private schools. My time at SDLC was ablsolutely amazing and I am incredibly grateful to have been able to take part in such a transformative experience.
